Why Hire a College Student for your Project?
The two big advantages of hiring students are that they're cheap and smart.

If you're thinking of hiring someone, it's helpful to think about what kind of person you would be comfortable working with. Maybe you like extroverts, or people who are good at getting things done, or people who are good at solving problems — whatever. It helps to look for those qualities first and foremost because if you don't find them, it's unlikely that anything else can make up for the difference.

Our company hires students because they are the future. They are young and fresh, have new ideas that help our company grow and make it easy to work with them. Challenge their skills by putting them in different projects that you think are out of their comfort zone. This will make them learn, adapt, and grow professionally to help advance your company forward.

How much experience does one need? Not much. I hired many people right out of college, and I've hired a few seasoned professionals as well. I've found that the experienced ones tend to be more careful about checking details — but they also tend to have set ideas about how things ought to be done and are less willing and able to deal with ambiguity or uncertainty. Which skill is more important depends on the company and the role. But in general, you get more value per person-year by going with an inexperienced person who has the other skills than by hiring a very experienced person who lacks them.

On the other hand, students develop quickly and experience more changes within a few months than people do in years. Their outlook is refreshing, and they bring both quantitative and cognitive potential, which makes them unique and rare.

As a startup CEO, I hired people right out of college partly because that's who applied to work at startups and partly because I wouldn't say I liked working with people who had been made so cynical by experience that they insisted on having a "realistic" view of everything.

But, we mainly hired them because they are smart and got the job done. And that's all you need to know about hiring: most candidates can learn whatever skills they lack if they're smart enough, and if some can't, you don't want them anyway.
